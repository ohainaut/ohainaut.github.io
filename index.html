I am an astronomer working at ESO, the European Southern Observatory. This is my personal page, very similar to my work page.

Science

Nice Results

  • The first interstellar asteroid, 1I/2017U1 Oumuamua, passed in front of our telescopes. More at ESO, IfA, Gemini, CFHT, NASA, and in Nature. We also studied its complex rotation and detected non-gravitational acceleration, likely from cometary activity, published in Nature.
  • Weird tail-less comets (the Manxes) are coming from the Oort Cloud. Project and results at Gemini, ESO, IfA, and CFHT.
  • Halley's Comet was observed for the last time in March 2003 at the distance of Neptune, and was APOD on October 3.
  • Comet Hale-Bopp was still very active in 2001. A color image is available here.
  • Discovery and analysis work includes 2000 OF8, 1996 TO66, supernova SN 1998 bw, comet Hyakutake, TNO 1994 TG2, and Pluto/Charon imaging.

Software Tools

  • A series of mildly useful scripts and tools on my GitHub.
  • Online satellite constellation simulators for evaluating the impact on astronomical observations, and the source code.
  • Online Astrolabe Generator and its source code.
  • MeteoMonitor for La Silla, and Garching.
  • POS1/Astromet for astrometric calibration using MIDAS (was in advance on its time, now very obsolete).
  • TMAG for photometric calibration using MIDAS (very obsolete).
  • All-sky camera:: the original La Silla one, completely obsolete, still working on La Silla (LASC). Then a custom and state-of-the-art one was installed on Paranal by Apical - here is a browser for its archive.
