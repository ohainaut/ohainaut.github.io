Comet Hale-Bopp

I'm working on it!

UH88'' at Mauna-Kea

Nov. 8, 1996


This image of Comet Hale-Bopp was obtained on Nov. 8, 1996 on the UH 88" telescope at Mauna Kea by J. Bauer, O.R. Hainaut and C.C. Petersen (larger version here). The comet was very low on the horizon, and we took the image through some clouds just a few minutes before we had to close the dome because of high humidity.

The detector is a huge CCD mosaic, composed of 8 individual chips; the thick black lines correspond to the gaps between them. This short "R" exposure has been processed in order to enhance the jets.

For more information on Comet Hale-Bopp, go to the Hale and Bopp's (the discoverers) page, or the well documented ESO Hale-Bopp page.
